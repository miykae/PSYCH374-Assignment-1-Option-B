Educational Module

What is Pavlovian Conditioning?

When a neutral cue (like a light or sound) is repeatedly paired with a reward, we learn that the cue predicts the reward. This is classical conditioning — the same process Pavlov studied with dogs.

Sign-Tracking vs Goal-Tracking

Sign-trackers are drawn to the cue itself — the signal that predicts reward. The cue becomes a "motivational magnet" that captures attention and approach. They interact with the cue even though the reward appears elsewhere.

Goal-trackers focus on the reward location. They use the cue only as a predictor and move toward where the reward will appear.

Both groups learn the association equally well — the difference is where they direct their attention and behaviour.

Dopamine & Cue Reactivity

Dopamine in the brain's reward system (especially the nucleus accumbens) helps assign incentive salience to cues — making them "wanted" and attention-grabbing. Sign-trackers show stronger dopamine-driven responses to reward cues. Goal-trackers rely more on cortical, goal-directed processing.

Relationship to Addiction Vulnerability

In animal models, sign-trackers show greater impulsivity, cue-induced relapse, and vulnerability to addiction-related behaviours. However, this is a risk factor, not a destiny. Many sign-trackers never develop addiction. Goal-tracking tendencies can be strengthened through practice and awareness.

Behavioural Safeguards

This task includes built-in safeguards designed to support goal-directed behaviour and reduce over-reliance on cues. Here's what we have in place:

Goal-Tracking Training Prompts

If you click the star (cue) 3 times in a row, a gentle prompt will appear: "Tip: Try going for just the gift box instead!" This encourages you to redirect attention toward the reward rather than the cue.

Cue Salience Reduction

After the tip appears, if you click the cue 3 more times in a row, the system automatically reduces the cue's prominence: the star's sound is turned off and its appearance is dimmed. This helps make the cue less attention-grabbing so you can practice focusing on the gift box.

Reflective Feedback

After the task, you'll receive balanced feedback about your response pattern. The feedback explains what your pattern might mean without pathologizing, and reminds you that both sign-tracking and goal-tracking are normal variations. It also suggests ways to practice goal-directed attention in daily life if you wish.
